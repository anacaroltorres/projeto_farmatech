DIGITAL TRANSFORMATION FOR A MORE CONNECTED HEALTH SYSTEM.

WHAT IS E-HEALTH?

E-health refers to the use of digital technologies such as the internet, mobile devices and software applications to deliver healthcare services and manage health-related information. It encompasses a variety of activities, including telemedicine, which allows remote consultations between patients and healthcare professionals via video calls, phone, or messaging; Health Information Systems (HIS), which digitize the management of patient records, appointments, billing and other administrative tasks; mobile health (mHealth), which involves apps and wearable devices to monitor vital signs, track physical activity and provide medication reminders; Electronic Health Records (EHR), which are digital versions of patient charts that allow real-time access to medical information by healthcare providers; and data analytics and Big Data, which use large datasets and artificial intelligence to improve diagnoses, treatments, and healthcare management. E-health helps increase the efficiency, accessibility and quality of healthcare services, promoting preventive care, patient engagement, and personalized medicine.

HOW DOES E-COMMERCE WORKS?

E-commerce (or electronic commerce) is the process of buying and selling goods and services over the internet. This process begins when a consumer visits an e-commerce site, explores different products, and adds items to their shopping cart. At checkout, they provide their shipping details and payment information to complete the transaction. Once payment is processed, the seller prepares the order for shipment, and the products are delivered to the customer's address. E-commerce can be categorized as business-to-consumer (B2C), where businesses sell to individuals, or consumer-to-consumer (C2C), where individuals sell to one another through online platforms. In the pharmaceutical area, E-commerce can also be extremely useful.

TELEPHARMACY

Did you know pharmacists also offer consultations? With advancing technology, these can now be done online through teleconsultation, one of the four telepharmacy modalities. Telepharmacy uses technology to provide remote healthcare, health education, and administrative support. It includes:

  • Teleconsultation: Direct patient care to guide medication use and resolve treatment issues;
  • Telemonitoring: Remote monitoring of patient's health parameters;
  • Teleinterconsultation: Collaboration between pharmacists and other healthcare professionals to improve outcomes;
  • Teleconsulting: Professional discussions to clarify procedures or address work issues;
  • Telepharmacy, introduced in Brazil during the COVID-19 pandemic, expanded access to healthcare and highlighted role in promoting health. Despite challenges like internet access and cultural perceptions, telepharmacy, especially teleconsultation, offers personalized, humanized care—even remotely.

APPLICATIONS

Advancements in technology and demand for improved treatment adherence have led to the rise of pharmaceutical apps and online medication sales, transforming healthcare. Pharmaceutical apps, like Drugs.com, ProDoctor, and My a: care, help users with medication reminders, dosage tracking, drug interaction checks, and finding pharmacies. These tools improve treatment adherence, especially during the pandemic, by offering remote support and enabling follow-ups with healthcare professionals. Online medication sales have also surged, with Brazil leading in Latin America. E-commerce simplifies purchases, especially for unwell individuals, while licensed pharmacies ensure safety with qualified pharmacists and regulatory compliance. Strict rules govern the sale of prescription medications online, but this market continues to grow, driven by digital adoption and convenience. Both innovations enhance healthcare access and patient quality of life, with future advancements promising even greater functionality and accessibility.

AI IN HEALTH CARE

HOW IS AI RELATED WITH HEALTH SERVICES?

Artificial Intelligence (AI) is transforming the healthcare industry, bringing new possibilities for diagnostics, treatment and operational efficiency. AI tools are being integrated into various aspects of healthcare to improve outcomes, reduce errors, and enhance patient care.

  • AI for Diagnostics AI-powered systems can analyze vast amounts of medical data, such as imaging, lab results, and patient histories, to assist in diagnosing diseases. Machine learning algorithms can detect patterns in medical images, helping radiologists identify conditions such as cancers, fractures, or neurological disorders more accurately and quickly. These tools are particularly beneficial in early diagnosis, leading to better patient outcomes and timely interventions.
  • Personalized Treatment One of AI’s most exciting contributions to healthcare is its ability to create personalized treatment plans. By analyzing data from genetic profiles, lifestyle habits, and medical histories, AI can recommend the most effective treatments for individual patients. This personalized approach ensures that patients receive the most suitable care, reducing the risk of adverse effects and increasing the chances of success.
  • Healthcare Automation AI is streamlining administrative tasks in healthcare, from patient scheduling to billing and insurance processing. This automation reduces the workload on healthcare providers, allowing them to focus more on patient care. Additionally, AI-driven chatbot and virtual assistants are improving patient communication by answering queries, sending reminders for medication, and providing basic health advice, creating a more efficient healthcare environment.

In conclusion, AI is playing a pivotal role in shaping the future of healthcare. By enhancing diagnostic accuracy, personalizing treatment, and automating routine tasks, AI is improving healthcare outcomes and accessibility, paving the way for a smarter and more efficient healthcare system.

ENVIRONMENT SUSTAINABILITY

Environmental sustainability is a topic that has become increasingly relevant. In 1987, the UN defined sustainability as meeting the needs of the present generation without compromising the ability of future generations to meet their own needs. Bearing this in mind, it is necessary to address other essential concepts for environmental sustainability, such as sustainable development and sustainability itself. In summary, sustainable development can be classified as the expectation that a country enters a phase of growth that is maintained over time, along with permanent economic expansion, leading to improved social indicators and environmental preservation.

Generally, sustainable development is associated with public policies, while sustainability can be defined as the ability to self-sustain. considering a digital pharmacy, its impact on environmental sustainability can be observed in several aspects, such as the resources preserved. A physical location requires many resources, including water, energy, employee transportation, and plastic waste, among others. Additionally, an online pharmacy can maintain an optimized inventory. Since products do not need to be displayed for customers, this could prevent waste or loss of medications. More sustainable packaging could also be implemented, as deliveries would be swift and handled by the company itself, eliminating the need for plastic bags or similar materials.
